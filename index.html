1. Design and Manufacturing Related Pojects

End-Effector Design for a Robotic Cotton Harvester

In this project, I designed an end-effector for a robotic cotton harvester, focusing on harvesting multiple cotton bolls concurrently. The end-effector is equipped with a roller featuring several tines, which efficiently draws in cotton fibers upon contact with opened cotton bolls. Subsequently, the gathered cotton fibers are conveyed to a collection chamber through a vacuum hose. This particular version of the end-effector achieved promising results, with a harvesting time of 5.52 seconds per boll and a picking ratio of 40.75% in field conditions. Notably, the end-effector's harvesting speed outpaces that of other robotic harvesters significantly. We are actively engaged in refining the end-effector for an even better picking ratio.

Design of Pigweed Pulling End-Effector (a Proof of Concept)

In this project, a concept for a Pigweed Pulling End-effector was developed. This specialized tool is designed to efficiently remove pigweed, a type of weed, from fields. The end-effector features a pair of rollers that rotate in opposite directions. These rollers are equipped with silicone covers, enhancing their ability to grip onto the weed. The ultimate purpose of this innovation is to attach the end-effector to a robotic arm on a rover, which will then be deployed to pull pigweed from cotton and peanut fields.

Design of Plant Height Measurement Tool

In many agricultural studies, recording plant height is a crucial data point. To address this need, I developed a specialized tool for measuring plant height. This tool features acrylic panels positioned on both sides of a slider, which facilitates easy reading of measurements on a Yardstick. The slider incorporates a screw mechanism, allowing for secure attachment and adjustment on the Yardstick. Additionally, a horizontal leveling bar is provided to ensure accurate alignment with the top part of the plant. This innovative tool simplifies the process of capturing plant height data in crop field research. We are using this tool in our cotton field research to record the plants height.

Design of Alcohol Sensing Sampling Device

A correlation exists between the stressed condition of plants and the release of alcohol from their bark. When plants are stressed and produce alcohol, this attracts certain types of insects that can harm the plant. Research into these scenarios requires quantifying the alcohol production in these stressed trees. To address this need, an alcohol-sensing sampling device was developed. This device incorporates alcohol sensors such as Solid Phase Microextraction fiber (SPME), a Drager Alcohol Sensor, and an Alcohol Sensing Card. With the utilization of this device, researchers can now sample alcohol production in stressed trees within 15 minutes, a substantial improvement compared to the traditional method that took around 3 hours. The assessment of the device's efficacy is currently underway.

Design of Rapis Brick Dust Conveying Machine

This was our undergraduate final year project, which involved a team of four members. We designed and constructed a pneumatic conveying machine for transporting brick dust (known as 'rapis'). Brick dust is commonly used for insulating the upper part of brick furnaces, and traditionally, it was manually transported by workers. However, this manual transport was not only dusty but also hazardous to human health. Our project aimed to replace manual transportation with an automated machine system using a pneumatic setup. This system consisted of a hopper for loading the brick dust, an air rotary valve for controlled feeding, a blower to generate pneumatic pressure, and a hose leading to a cyclone separator.

2. Projects Related to Sensors and Control Systems

A Prototype of Peanut Replanter

In the context of the Sensor and Transducer Course project, I developed a Replanter Prototype designed to identify gaps in plant seedling distribution and subsequently reseed those areas. The prototype operates by utilizing cost-effective sensors: an Ultrasound Sensor to identify gaps and a Rotary Encoder to quantify the distance covered by the prototype. The microcontroller plays a crucial role in interpreting the sensor data and controlling the servo motor to carry out the reseeding process.

Zone Based Variable Rate Nitrogen Prescription


In this project, a corn field was divided into two zones with different yield potentials based on elevation, terrain, and soil conductivity variables of the field. Subsequently, a variable rate fertilizer application was prescribed to reduce costs and achieve the yield potential. UAN28% was used as the Nitrogen source. The required in-season UAN28% rates for high and low-yield zones were 54.1 gal/ac and 50.5 gal/ac, respectively.

A Web Server with Web API


In this project, we built a web server with a Web API to store agricultural sensor data like temperature and humidity from various locations. This data is instantly accessible online. We created a database using SQLiteDatabaseBrowser64 to store location, temperature, humidity, and timestamps. FastAPI was used for the web API, hosted on a server.For continuous data storage, an Arduino Uno collected analog sensor values and communicated with a local computer via serial ports. A Python program on the computer retrieved sensor data via serial port and stored it in the server database.
